Compiled from TAG's public grading rubric on 2026-04-08.

## Scope

This file summarizes TAG's published descriptive rubric for every grade tier from `1` through `10 Pristine` (`10P` in collector shorthand). It is a **paraphrased research document**, not a verbatim copy of TAG's rubric text.

## Rubric Summary By Grade

### 10P | TAG Pristine | 990-1000

- Centering: front about `51/49`; back about `54.5/45.5` for sports or `52/48` for TCG.
- Corners: essentially flawless to the eye, with sharp geometry and almost no fill or fray even under high-resolution inspection.
- Surface: clean enough that only TAG's non-human-observable defects remain.
- Edges: essentially flawless, with only negligible microscopic fill or fray.
- Practical interpretation: TAG's top tier is not just "pack fresh"; it is a nearly ideal specimen under both normal viewing and imaging.

### 10 | Gem Mint | 950-989

- Centering: front about `55/45`; back about `70/30` for sports or `65/35` for TCG.
- Corners: still sharp and square, but minor edge/corner fiber disturbance can appear, especially on the back.
- Surface: extremely attractive overall; tiny pits, light scratches that do not break gloss, faint print/refractor lines, or minor print issues may be present.
- Edges: very light fill, fray, or edge wear can appear under magnification.
- Practical interpretation: elite card with visible imperfections only at close inspection or in imaging.

### 9 | Mint | 900-949

- Centering: front about `60/40`; back about `90/10` for sports or `75/25` for TCG.
- Corners: generally sharp, but light touches become easier to see, including limited front visibility and more back-corner disruption.
- Surface: deeper pits, multiple small pits, longer non-gloss-breaking scratches, full-length print/refractor lines, minor print spots, or back scratches can appear.
- Edges: minor but visible wear can affect one or two edges, with more noticeable fill/fray under hi-res review.
- Practical interpretation: still premium, but no longer "nearly flawless" once examined carefully.

### 8.5 | Near Mint-Mint+ | 850-899

- Centering: front about `62.5/37.5`; back about `95/5` for sports or `85/15` for TCG.
- Corners: still square-looking overall, but multiple light front touches are visible and some stock loss can show on back corners.
- Surface: multiple defects are now present together, including gloss-breaking scratches, multiple print/refractor lines, slight scuffing, or subtle focus issues.
- Edges: visible wear or light chipping can affect multiple edges, with increasing fray.
- Practical interpretation: strong eye appeal remains, but the card has clearly entered the "multi-defect" zone.

### 8 | Near Mint-Mint | 800-849

- Centering: front about `65/35`; back remains within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: one corner may begin to show mild wear while other corners keep a roughly square shape with light touches; back-corner wear increases.
- Surface: several prior defects can combine, and larger print issues or minor roller lines may show.
- Edges: wear and light chipping are now present on all four edges; slight edge lifting can begin.
- Practical interpretation: high-end near mint card, but imperfections are no longer isolated.

### 7.5 | Near Mint+ | 750-799

- Centering: front about `67.5/32.5`; back still must avoid miscut/neighbor-card exposure.
- Corners: all four corners may show touching and some loss of sharpness; slight fraying begins to register on the corner surfaces.
- Surface: minor denting can appear, and multiple visible flaws may reach more important visual regions.
- Edges: notching, edge lifting, chipping, and general wear become plainly visible.
- Practical interpretation: solid collectible condition with obvious wear beginning to shape the grade.

### 7 | Near Mint | 700-749

- Centering: front about `70/30`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: corners remain mostly square, but several now show obvious fraying; slight bends or hits can affect the surface around the corner.
- Surface: a minor dent may affect both front and back, and scattered print-dot defects become visible.
- Edges: all four edges can show lifting, visible wear, and chipping; small notches may appear.
- Practical interpretation: the card is still respectable, but wear is now easy to spot without magnification.

### 6.5 | Excellent-Mint+ | 650-699

- Centering: front about `72.5/27.5`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: a corner can start to lose shape from stronger wear, bends, dings, or fill; three or four corners may show significant fray or chipping.
- Surface: worsening defects can include short wrinkles from an edge and small areas of water damage on the back.
- Edges: two edges may show notching, with visible fray and enough fill to create slight waviness.
- Practical interpretation: no longer near mint in feel; structural wear is beginning to alter shape and texture.

### 6 | Excellent-Mint | 600-649

- Centering: front about `75/25`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: one corner may show slight rounding, while several corners exhibit stronger front/back wear and a clear bend or ding.
- Surface: longer wrinkles, light staining, larger or multiple dents, more concentrated scuffing, and noticeable focus problems can appear.
- Edges: three or more edges may show notches, or one edge may have a major notch; chipping and roughness become prominent.
- Practical interpretation: moderate wear with clear structural and print/surface problems.

### 5.5 | Excellent+ | 550-599

- Centering: front about `77.5/22.5`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: one or two corners show visible light rounding, and stronger bends or slight wrinkles can affect corner surfaces.
- Surface: small front wrinkles, multiple back wrinkles, visible front staining, and localized water damage can appear.
- Edges: at least two edges may show severe notching, with larger chipped sections affecting the card surface.
- Practical interpretation: visible wear has become significant and is affecting multiple defect classes at once.

### 5 | Excellent | 500-549

- Centering: front about `80/20`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: one or two corners become more rounded, while all four lose crisp squareness from wear, dings, fill, or chipping.
- Surface: multiple small front wrinkles, larger staining areas, multiple scuffed regions, and noticeable gloss loss are expected.
- Edges: notches, chipping, fill, and roughness become major visual factors on the same edge or across edges.
- Practical interpretation: eye appeal is clearly compromised, though the card remains intact and identifiable.

### 4.5 | Very Good-Excellent+ | 450-499

- Centering: front about `82.5/17.5`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: at least one corner is clearly rounded, with multiple corners also showing bends, dings, fraying, or chipping.
- Surface: minor creasing can break stock, and larger wrinkles may intrude into more important facial/image regions.
- Edges: roughness worsens, and multiple edges show distracting chipping, notches, and fray.
- Practical interpretation: the card crosses from "worn" into plainly damaged territory.

### 4 | Very Good-Excellent | 400-449

- Centering: front about `85/15`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: two or three corners are clearly rounded, while bends, dings, fraying, chipping, and surface disruption keep escalating.
- Surface: a crease may be visible from both sides; larger wrinkles, heavier staining, and broader water damage can appear.
- Edges: edge wear is obvious, with exposed and dirty underlying stock.
- Practical interpretation: moderate-to-heavy wear with multiple plainly visible defects.

### 3.5 | Very Good+ | 350-399

- Centering: front about `87.5/12.5`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: all four corners are rounded, and bends/dings grow more obvious while corner surfaces deteriorate further.
- Surface: long wrinkles, multiple minor creases, stronger scuffing, and wear severe enough to hinder text or stat readability on the back can appear.
- Edges: persistent wear produces clearly visible filled areas from heavy handling.
- Practical interpretation: the card remains complete, but condition issues are now broad and immediately apparent.

### 3 | Very Good | 300-349

- Centering: front about `90/10`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: all four corners are rounded toward extreme rounding, with dirty fraying and stronger bends that damage the corner surfaces.
- Surface: heavier wrinkles or creases can appear, including long horizontal or vertical wrinkles, and much of the original gloss may be gone.
- Edges: severe fill, fray, notching, chipping, dirtiness, and roughness are present.
- Practical interpretation: heavily worn card with strong structural and cosmetic degradation.

### 2.5 | Good+ | 250-299

- Centering: front about `92.5/7.5`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: up to all four corners can show extreme rounding, enough to push beyond the corner area and start affecting the edges.
- Surface: heavier wrinkles/creases, strong print and focus defects, staining, and scuffing severe enough to remove words or blur image detail can appear.
- Edges: one or more edge defects begin to migrate deeper into the card surface from an edge.
- Practical interpretation: major wear is now affecting readability, image recognition, and structure.

### 2 | Good | 200-249

- Centering: front about `95/5`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: all four corners are moving into extreme rounding with visible dirt accumulation.
- Surface: multiple full-length wrinkles or heavier creases can persist; pinholes or minor glue on the reverse become possible.
- Edges: edge damage pushes farther into the card from multiple sides, and a minor tear can appear.
- Practical interpretation: substantial damage is present, but the card is still generally whole.

### 1.5 | Fair | 150-199

- Centering: front about `98.33/1.67`; back still within the tiny-border/no-miscut rule.
- Corners: corner rounding worsens further, to the point that fibers may appear to be separating or hanging on.
- Surface: multiple major issues can coexist, and staple holes or tape residue on the back become possible.
- Edges: defects can intrude deeply from all edges; image loss, multiple tears, or a more significant single tear may exist.
- Practical interpretation: severe damage with clear structural compromise.

### 1 | Poor | 100-149

- Centering: the card may show only a tiny sliver of border, but cannot be miscut or show part of another card.
- Corners: corners are not merely rounded; they can be misshapen, torn, or missing material from heavy wear and handling.
- Surface: multiple major flaws can combine, and even tape or glue on the front is possible.
- Edges: all severe defects are in play, including heavy wear, image loss near edges, and larger tears.
- Practical interpretation: lowest TAG rubric tier while still avoiding true miscut/neighbor-card exposure.

## Progression Notes

- TAG's rubric is cumulative. Lower grades generally include the worsening continuation of defect types introduced above them.
- Centering tolerance broadens almost every half grade on the front, while the back remains tightly constrained by a "tiny sliver of border allowed, but no miscut/other-card exposure" rule through most of the scale.
- The defect progression moves from microscopic or imaging-only issues at the top, to visible wear, then to structural damage, staining, wrinkles, creases, tears, stock loss, and obstruction of card image/text at the bottom.
- `10P` is a collector shorthand convenience; TAG's published page labels that top tier as `10` with the descriptor `TAG Pristine`.

## Research Use Notes

- Useful for calibration: mapping observed defects to likely rubric bands before DIG-level scoring.
- Useful for prompt engineering: describing grade bands in defect-language rather than only numeric score-language.
- Not enough on its own to reproduce TAG scoring because the public rubric is descriptive and does not expose TAG's internal weighting, compounding rules, or region-level scoring math.
