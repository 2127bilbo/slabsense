# BGS Card Grading Standards

## Overview
BGS (Beckett Grading Services) uses a 10-point scale with half-point increments. BGS is known for being **stricter** than PSA/CGC, especially on centering. They display **4 subgrades** on every label.

---

## Grade Scale & Names

| Grade | Official Name | Description |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| 10 | Pristine | Virtually perfect (Black Label = all 10s; Gold Label = three 10s + one 9.5) |
| 9.5 | Gem Mint | Near perfect with only minor, imperceptible flaws |
| 9 | Mint | Sharp and clean but with minor flaws |
| 8.5 | Near Mint-Mint+ | Minor wear visible on close inspection |
| 8 | Near Mint-Mint (NM-MT) | Minor visible wear, no major flaws |
| 7.5 | Near Mint+ | Light wear with minor issues |
| 7 | Near Mint (NM) | Light wear visible but overall sharp |
| 6.5 | Excellent-Mint+ | Minor to moderate wear |
| 6 | Excellent-Mint (EX-MT) | Minor visible wear |
| 5.5 | Excellent+ | Moderate wear |
| 5 | Excellent (EX) | Some wear, still decent condition |
| 4 | Very Good-Excellent | Noticeable wear |
| 3 | Very Good (VG) | Moderate wear but collectible |
| 2 | Good | Heavy wear but intact |
| 1 | Poor | Severe wear, barely collectible |

---

## The Four BGS Subgrades

Every BGS label shows these four scores (1-10 with half points):

1. **Centering** - Image alignment within borders
2. **Corners** - Shape and wear of all four corners
3. **Edges** - Smoothness, chipping, fraying, layering
4. **Surface** - Scratches, print lines, gloss, clarity

---

## Centering Requirements

**BGS is stricter than competitors** - requires 50/50 for a perfect 10.

| Grade | Front Centering | Back Centering |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| **10** | 50/50 (perfect) | 50/50 (perfect) |
| **9.5** | 55/45 or better | 60/40 or better |
| **9** | 60/40 or better | 65/35 or better |
| **8.5** | 62/38 or better | 70/30 or better |
| **8** | 65/35 or better | 75/25 or better |
| **7** | 70/30 or better | 80/20 or better |
| **6** | 75/25 or better | 85/15 or better |

---

## Corner Subgrade Specifications

| Grade | Specifications |
|-------|----------------|
| **10** | All four corners razor sharp, zero imperfections under magnification |
| **9.5** | One corner with slightest imperfection; other three perfect |
| **9** | Minor wear on one or two corners, slight whitening on one acceptable |
| **8.5** | Light wear on multiple corners, slight rounding possible |
| **8** | Clear wear or creases, rounding on one or more corners |
| **7** | Edge/corner whitening visible on close inspection |

**Key**: Any whitening, fraying, or softness automatically drops below 10.

---

## Edge Subgrade Specifications

| Grade | Specifications |
|-------|----------------|
| **10** | Flawless, razor-sharp, no chipping/notching/fraying/layering |
| **9.5** | Slight roughness or minor chipping on one edge only |
| **9** | Minor edge wear, slight roughness acceptable |
| **8.5** | Moderate roughness or minor notching acceptable |
| **8** | Noticeable wear, moderate chipping possible |
| **7** | Noticeable roughness, slight notching acceptable |
| **6** | Readily chipped/notched and/or slightly layered |

**Note**: Dark-bordered cards receive stricter scrutiny.

---

## Surface Subgrade Specifications

| Grade | Specifications |
|-------|----------------|
| **10** | Zero print spots, perfect gloss, zero scratches, flawless |
| **9.5** | One minor spot barely noticeable, excellent gloss |
| **9** | A handful of printing specks OR one minor spot, one faint print line allowed |
| **8.5** | Minor print spots, very minor scratches only under close inspection |
| **8** | Few minor print spots, most original gloss, minor scratches under magnification |
| **7** | Little to no scratches visible at 16 inches, most gloss retained |

---

## How Final Grade is Calculated

**NOT a simple average** - BGS uses weighted algorithm emphasizing lowest subgrade.

### Primary Rule
**Final grade can only be 0.5 higher than your lowest subgrade.**

Exception: If two+ subgrades share lowest score, final typically equals that lowest.

### Examples

| Subgrades (C/Co/E/S) | Final | Explanation |
|----------------------|-------|-------------|
| 10/10/10/9.5 | **10** | Lowest is 9.5, can be 0.5 higher |
| 9.5/9.5/9.5/9 | **9.5** | Lowest is 9, can be 0.5 higher |
| 10/10/9/10 | **9.5** | Lowest is 9, can be 0.5 higher |
| 9/8.5/8/9.5 | **8.5** | Lowest is 8, can be 0.5 higher |
| 9.5/9.5/7/9.5 | **8.5-9** | Significant drop in one subgrade |

## Special Labels

### Black Label (Introduced 2014)
- **Requirement**: ALL four subgrades must be perfect 10
- **Rarity**: Less than 1% of all BGS 10s
- **Value**: Significantly higher than Gold Label

### Gold Label - BGS 10 Pristine
- **Requirement**: Three 10s and one 9.5
- **Rarity**: ~15-20% of BGS 10s

### Gold Label - BGS 9.5 Gem Mint
- **Requirement**: High subgrade mix (9.5s and 10s)
- More common than BGS 10

## Key Differences from Other Companies

| Aspect | BGS | PSA | CGC |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|
| Subgrades shown | Yes (4) | No | No |
| Centering for 10 | 50/50 | 55/45 | 50/50 (Pristine) |
| Half points | Yes | Yes (no 9.5) | Yes |
| Strictest on | Centering | — | — |
