# CGC Card Grading - Defect Weights & Scoring Impact

## Overview

CGC (Certified Guaranty Company) uses **holistic grading** without published subgrades. They evaluate centering, corners, edges, and surface together but do NOT follow a strict "lowest factor" methodology like BGS.

**Key Philosophy:** CGC believes poor centering alone (if not drastically poor) should not prevent an otherwise near-mint card from achieving 8.5+.

---

## Holistic vs Lowest Factor Approach

### CGC's Unique Philosophy
- NOT a strict "lowest factor" system (unlike BGS)
- CGC is more lenient than BGS on centering relative to other factors
- Excellent conditions in corners, edges, and surface **CAN compensate** for slightly off-centering

### Example
- A card with mediocre centering (65/35) but flawless corners, edges, and surface **CAN** achieve 8.5-9 at CGC
- The same card with BGS might cap at 8.5 or 8 due to "lowest factor"

---

## Pristine 10 vs Gem Mint 10 (The Crucial Difference)

### Pristine 10 (Highest Elite)
| Attribute | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Centering** | 50/50 (perfect) front AND back |
| **Corners** | Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ under 10x magnification |
| **Edges** | Perfect, virtually flawless under intense scrutiny |
| **Surface** | Free of ANY print spots, perfect gloss |
| **Defects** | NONE visible |

### Gem Mint 10 (Elite)
| Attribute | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Centering** | 55/45 front, 75/25 back |
| **Corners** | Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ under 10x |
| **Edges** | Perfect to naked eye |
| **Surface** | Free of print spots, perfect gloss |
| **Defects** | One criterion doesn't meet Pristine 10 standards (usually centering) |

### The Real Difference
**Centering is the PRIMARY differentiator** between Pristine 10 and Gem Mint 10. A card with perfect 50/50 centering can achieve Pristine, while 55/45 results in Gem Mint.

---

## Centering Standards by Grade

| Grade | Front Centering | Back Centering |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Pristine 10** | 50/50 | 50/50 |
| **Gem Mint 10** | 55/45 | 75/25 |
| **9.5/9 Mint+/Mint** | 60/40 | 90/10 |
| **8.5/8 NM+/NM** | 65/35 | 65/35 |
| **7.5/7** | 65/35 or 70/30 | 70/30 |
| **6.5/6** | Beyond 70/30 | - |

**Critical:** Even perfect cards cannot achieve Pristine 10 with 55/45 centering - that's the hard cap.

---

## Specific Defect Impacts

### Perfect Card with 55/45 Centering
- **Grade:** Gem Mint 10 (not Pristine 10)
- The slight centering off prevents Pristine classification

### Microscopic Corner Whitening (invisible to naked eye)
- **Grade:** 9 or 9.5 maximum (prevents 10)
- Even microscopic corner wear visible only under 10x magnification drops from 10 to 9

### Light Surface Scratch
- **Grade:** 8.5 or 8 (depending on severity)
- **At 9:** One minor spot/surface defect allowed
- **At 8:** One minor flaw including subtle scratches allowed
- Multiple or obvious scratches: 8 or lower

### Print Spot/Speck
- **Grade:** 8.5-9 range (if light)
- **Single minor at 9:** Allowed
- **Pristine/Gem Mint 10:** ZERO print spots permitted
- Multiple spots: 8 or lower

### Light Surface Gloss Loss
- **Grade:** 8.5 or lower
- **Pristine/Gem Mint 10:** Requires perfect gloss
- **Mint 9:** Must have "all original gloss"

## Corner Grading Standards

| Grade | Corner Condition |
|-------|------------------|
| **Pristine/Gem Mint 10** | Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ under 10x magnification |
| **9-9.5** | Perfect to naked eye, may reveal slight imperfections at 10x |
| **8-8.5** | Sharp to naked eye, minor imperfections under magnification |
| **Below 8** | Visible wear to naked eye, rounding/fuzzing apparent |

**Critical rule:** Even microscopic corner whitening (invisible to naked eye) can drop a card from 10 to 9.

---

## Edge Grading Standards

**Weight in overall grade:** LOWEST priority after centering and surface.

| Grade | Edge Condition |
|-------|----------------|
| **Pristine/Gem Mint 10** | Perfect, virtually flawless under intense scrutiny |
| **9-9.5** | Slight minor wear visible on some edges |
| **8-8.5** | Relatively smooth with only minor touches of wear |
| **Below 8** | Rough or rough-cut edges, chipping, whitening |

---

## Surface Factors

CGC evaluates these surface elements:
- Scratches (light to heavy)
- Print spots (single specks to multiple)
- Ink spots and splotches
- Compressions and indentations
- Holo scratches/damage
- Gloss loss (critical)
- Silvering (edge whitening - CGC is stricter than PSA)

### Surface Gloss Requirements
| Grade | Gloss Requirement |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Pristine/Gem Mint 10** | Perfect gloss, no loss |
| **Mint 9** | All original gloss; one minor spot allowed |
| **8.5** | Original gloss mostly intact |
| **8** | Most original colors and gloss intact |
| **Below 8** | Increasing gloss loss acceptable |

---

## Crease & Major Defect Rules

### Visible Crease (ink break/color break)
- **Grade cap:** 7 or lower (typically)
- Tiny creases: Almost always automatic 7 or lower if visible from front
- Visible fold/bend: Typically caps at 5 or lower

### Factory/Production Defects
- CGC grades differently based on defect severity and prevalence in that set
- Print lines (horizontal lines): Impact depends on frequency in set
- Rough edges (Jungle set): CGC accounts for as-issued defects
- Does NOT automatically reject cards but reduces grades accordingly

## Grade Boundaries (Detailed)

### CGC 10 Pristine
- Centering: 50/50 front, 50/50 reverse
- Corners: Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ at 10x
- Edges: Perfect, virtually flawless
- Surface: Free of ANY print spots, perfect gloss
- Allowable: NONE visible

### CGC 10 Gem Mint
- Centering: 55/45 front, 75/25 reverse
- Corners: Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ at 10x
- Edges: Perfect to naked eye
- Surface: Free of print spots, perfect gloss
- Allowable: One criterion doesn't meet Pristine standards

### CGC 9.5 Mint Plus
- Centering: 60/40 front, 90/10 reverse
- Corners: Sharp to naked eye, minor imperfections at 10x
- Edges: Slight minor wear allowed
- Surface: Free of print spots, all original gloss
- Allowable: One minor spot or surface defect
- Feature: Premium eye appeal

### CGC 9 Mint
- Centering: 60/40 front, 90/10 reverse
- Corners: Sharp to naked eye, slight imperfections at 10x allowed
- Edges: Slight minor flaws visible
- Surface: All original gloss; one minor spot/defect
- Allowable: One of: slight edge/corner wear, very minor scratches, slight off-center, slight surface traces

### CGC 8.5 NM Plus
- Centering: 65/35 or better
- Corners: Slight imperfections under magnification
- Edges: Slight minor wear visible
- Surface: Minor scratches, subtle print spots allowed
- Allowable: Touch of wear on 2-3 corners or slightly rough edges

### CGC 8 NM
- Centering: 65/35 or better
- Corners: Sharp to naked eye, slight imperfections at 10x
- Edges: Minor touches of wear
- Surface: Minor scratches, subtle print spots, focus imperfections
- Allowable: One minor flaw (corner imperfections, subtle print spots)

## CGC's AI Technology

### What's Automated
- Centering measurement (pixel-level border analysis)
- Card attribution
- Alteration/counterfeit detection (UV/infrared)

### What's NOT Automated
- Final grade assignment (human graders make final call)
- Subjective factors like eye appeal
- Surface assessment (still requires human judgment)

## CGC vs BGS vs PSA Comparison

| Aspect | CGC | BGS | PSA |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|
| Centering weight | Important but compensatable | Strictest | Moderate |
| Approach | Holistic | Lowest factor | Lowest factor |
| Centering for 10 | 50/50 (Pristine) / 55/45 (Gem) | 50/50 | 55/45 |
| Subgrades shown | No | Yes (4) | No |
| Back centering leniency | 75/25 for Gem Mint | 60/40 for 9.5 | 75/25 |

### Resale Value
- CGC 10 cards sell for 72-85% of equivalent PSA 10 prices
- Market favors PSA for high-end investment cards

---

## Key Distinctions: 9.5 vs 9

### CGC 9.5 Mint Plus
- Premium eye appeal and condition
- Exceptional centering, surface qualities, or color
- One minor spot/surface defect allowed
- Near-perfect corners and edges
- Higher market value

### CGC 9 Mint
- Good condition but allows more noticeable defects
- One of several permitted minor imperfections
- More tolerance for corner/edge wear
- Strong collectible grade but not premium

**Grade difference trigger:** 9.5 requires "premium eye appeal" whereas 9 is simply well-centered with minor acceptable defects.
