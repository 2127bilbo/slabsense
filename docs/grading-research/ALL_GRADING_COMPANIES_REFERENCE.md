Consolidated from the Markdown research files in this folder on 2026-04-08.

## Scope

This file combines the key material from the existing research docs for:

- PSA
- BGS
- SGC
- CGC
- TAG

For each company, this includes:

- grading scale and grade labels
- centering standards
- major defect/condition standards
- how scoring or weighting appears to work
- practical grading data notes

## PSA

### Standards

- Scale: `1-10` with half grades from `1.5` through `8.5`
- No `PSA 9.5`
- Overall style: single final grade, no subgrades shown
- Main criteria: centering, corners, edges, surface

### Grade Structure

| Grade | Label |
|-------|-------|
| 10 | Gem Mint |
| 9 | Mint |
| 8 | Near Mint-Mint |
| 7 | Near Mint |
| 6 | Excellent-Mint |
| 5 | Excellent |
| 4 | Very Good-Excellent |
| 3 | Very Good |
| 2 | Good |
| 1 | Poor |

Half grades available: `1.5, 2.5, 3.5, 4.5, 5.5, 6.5, 7.5, 8.5`

### Centering Standards

| Grade | Front | Back |
|-------|-------|------|
| 10 | 55/45 or better | 75/25 or better |
| 9 | 60/40 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 8 | 65/35 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 7 | 70/30 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 6 | 80/20 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 5 | 85/15 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 4 | 85/15 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 3 | 90/10 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 2 | 90/10 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 1 | 90/10 or better | 90/10 or better |

### Condition Standards

- `PSA 10`: essentially perfect, no visible wear, no visible whitening, strong gloss, no meaningful surface issues
- `PSA 9`: one minor flaw is generally acceptable
- `PSA 8`: slight fraying at one or two corners, light print or surface issues
- `PSA 7-6`: visible but still moderate wear on corners, edges, or surface
- `PSA 5-3`: increasingly visible rounding, scratches, surface wear, and edge damage
- `PSA 2-1`: heavy wear, creasing, chipping, major damage

### Weighting / Scoring Behavior

- Research consensus in this folder: PSA behaves like a `lowest-score-wins` system
- The weakest of the four major categories typically caps the overall grade
- Not an averaging system
- Exact numerical weights are not public

### Practical Defect Impacts

- Any visible whitening generally prevents `PSA 10`
- Minor edge chipping often places a card in `PSA 8-9`
- One light surface scratch usually caps at `PSA 9`
- Any crease generally caps at `PSA 6` or below
- Multiple defects across categories compound the drop, but the worst category still dominates

### Special Labels / Qualifiers

| Qualifier | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Authentic | Genuine item without numeric grade |
| Authentic Altered | Genuine but altered |
| MK | Writing or ink marks |
| MC | Miscut / atypical cut |
| OC | Off-center |
| OF | Out of focus / out of register |
| ST | Staining |
| PD | Print defect |

### Key Notes

- PSA 10 front centering was tightened to `55/45` in Q1 2025 in the source docs
- No visible subgrades are shown on labels
- Half grades exist, but not `9.5`

## BGS

### Standards

- Scale: `1-10` with half grades, including `9.5`
- Known for strict grading, especially centering
- Shows `4 subgrades` on label:
  - centering
  - corners
  - edges
  - surface

### Grade Structure

| Grade | Label |
|-------|-------|
| 10 | Pristine |
| 9.5 | Gem Mint |
| 9 | Mint |
| 8.5 | Near Mint-Mint+ |
| 8 | Near Mint-Mint |
| 7.5 | Near Mint+ |
| 7 | Near Mint |
| 6.5 | Excellent-Mint+ |
| 6 | Excellent-Mint |
| 5.5 | Excellent+ |
| 5 | Excellent |
| 4 | Very Good-Excellent |
| 3 | Very Good |
| 2 | Good |
| 1 | Poor |

### Centering Standards

| Subgrade / Grade | Front | Back |
|------------------|-------|------|
| 10 | 50/50 | 50/50 |
| 9.5 | 55/45 or better | 60/40 or better |
| 9 | 60/40 or better | 65/35 or better |
| 8.5 | 62/38 or better | 70/30 or better |
| 8 | 65/35 or better | 75/25 or better |
| 7 | 70/30 or better | 80/20 or better |
| 6 | 75/25 or better | 85/15 or better |

### Condition Standards

- `10`: razor-sharp corners, flawless edges, no print spots, no scratches
- `9.5`: nearly perfect, maybe one tiny imperfection
- `9`: minor wear on one or two corners, one faint print line or minor spot possible
- `8.5-8`: light wear, moderate roughness, minor scratches or print issues
- `7-6`: visible whitening, roughness, notching, chipping, or layering

### Weighting / Scoring Behavior

- BGS uses a weighted subgrade model, not simple averaging
- Primary rule from the research docs:
  - final grade can only be `0.5` above the lowest subgrade
  - if two or more subgrades share the lowest score, final usually equals that lowest score
- A single dramatically weak subgrade can still drag the whole card down sharply
- Exact formula is not public

### Practical Defect Impacts

- A single dinged corner can drop the corner subgrade to `8` or lower
- Light edge whitening can push a card from `9.5/10` down into `9` or `8.5`
- Surface is treated as the most subjective subgrade and often blocks top grades
- Dark-bordered cards are penalized harder on edge/corner whitening

### Special Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Black Label | All four subgrades are `10` |
| Gold Label BGS 10 | Three `10`s and one `9.5` |
| BGS 9.5 | Standard Gem Mint tier |

### Key Notes

- BGS is the strictest company here on perfect centering, requiring true `50/50` for a `10`
- Black Label is the rarest top-end BGS outcome
- BGS is the only company in this set that visibly displays all four core subgrades

- Scale: `1-10` with half grades up to `9.5`
- Two types of `10`:

### Condition Standards

- `10 Pristine`: no visible wear, no print/refractor lines, perfect gloss, flawless appearance
- `10 Gem Mint`: sharp corners, no visible wear, only slight non-distracting print issue possible
- `9-9.5`: slight nick or tiny surface issue acceptable
- `8-8.5`: sharp to naked eye, slight wear under magnification
- `7-6`: slight wear, scratching, fuzzing, notching
- `5 and below`: increasing rounding, chipping, creasing, stains, structural damage

### Weighting / Scoring Behavior

- Research docs characterize SGC as a `lowest-factor` style grader
- Not an averaging system
- Back centering carries unusual importance relative to PSA
- Multiple minor defects compound downward quickly

### Practical Defect Impacts

- A card with excellent front centering but poor back centering can miss SGC top grades
- `Pristine 10` is extremely rare because it demands `50/50` on both sides
- Tiny corner/edge wear pushes cards out of the top tiers fast

### Special Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Gold Label | Reserved for `Pristine 10` |

### Key Notes

- SGC’s defining trait in this set is strict back centering
- The source docs note that half-point grades require centering of the next higher full grade
- Gold Label Pristine is materially more valuable than standard Gem Mint 10

## CGC

### Standards

- Scale: `1-10` with half grades including `9.5`
- Two `10` tiers:
  - `Pristine 10`
  - `Gem Mint 10`
- No visible subgrades on label
- Uses a more holistic grading style than BGS

### Grade Structure

| Grade | Label |
|-------|-------|
| 10 Pristine | Pristine |
| 10 Gem Mint | Gem Mint |
| 9.5 | Mint+ |
| 9 | Mint |
| 8.5 | NM/Mint+ |
| 8 | NM/Mint |
| 7.5 | NM- |
| 7 | NM |
| 6.5 | EX/MT+ |
| 6 | EX/MT |
| 5.5 | EX+ |
| 5 | EX |
| 4.5 | VG/EX+ |
| 4 | VG/EX |
| 3.5 | VG+ |
| 3 | VG |
| 2.5 | Good+ |
| 2 | Good |
| 1.5 | Fair |
| 1 | Poor |

### Centering Standards

| Grade | Front | Back |
|-------|-------|------|
| Pristine 10 | 50/50 | 50/50 |
| Gem Mint 10 | 55/45 or better | 75/25 or better |
| 9.5 Mint+ | 60/40 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 9 Mint | 60/40 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 8 NM/Mint | 65/35 or better | not clearly published in source docs |
| 6 Good | 75/25 or better | not clearly published in source docs |
| 3 VG | 90/10 or better | not clearly published in source docs |

### Condition Standards

- `Pristine 10`: flawless to naked eye, strong under `10x` magnification, perfect gloss, no print spots
- `Gem Mint 10`: one criterion short of Pristine, usually centering
- `9.5`: strong gloss, four sharp corners, slight minor edge or surface issue allowed
- `9`: minor corner or edge wear and one minor surface issue allowed
- `8`: relatively smooth edges, most gloss intact, one minor flaw allowed
- `3-2`: moderate to heavy creasing, rounded corners, scuffing, writing, heavy wear
- `1.5-1`: catastrophic flaws, missing surface/cardstock, severe crease or hole issues

### Weighting / Scoring Behavior

- CGC is described in the source docs as `holistic`, not strict lowest-factor like BGS
- Strong corners, edges, and surface can partially offset slightly worse centering
- Centering still matters, especially for separating `Pristine 10` from `Gem Mint 10`
- Exact math is not public

### Practical Defect Impacts

- `55/45` centering can still achieve `Gem Mint 10`, but not `Pristine 10`
- Microscopic corner whitening can drop a card from `10` to `9` territory
- One minor surface defect is acceptable around `9-9.5`
- Visible crease usually caps the card at `7` or lower
- Factory defects may be contextualized rather than treated identically in every set

### Special Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Gold Label | Pristine 10 only |

### Key Notes

- CGC is more forgiving on slightly off-centering than BGS
- CGC’s `Pristine 10` versus `Gem Mint 10` split is driven heavily by centering and perfect-surface expectations
- Human judgment remains central even with CGC automation for centering and detection

## TAG

### Standards

- Uses a `1000-point` internal score mapped to `1-10`
- Two top `10` bands:
  - `Pristine` = `990-1000`
  - `Gem Mint` = `950-989`
- No `9.5`
- Uses `8 subgrades`:
  - front centering
  - back centering
  - front corners
  - back corners
  - front surface
  - back surface
  - front edges
  - back edges
- Public DIG ecosystem includes defect annotations and score breakdowns

### Condition Standards

- `Pristine`: essentially flawless, only NHODs allowed
- `Gem Mint`: minor print imperfection, tiny pit, or light non-gloss-breaking scratch possible
- `Mint 9`: a few small pits/scratches, very light touches, minor print issues
- `8.5-8`: multiple visible but still moderate flaws, touched corners, light fraying, scuffing, print lines
- `7.5-7`: visible wear on multiple corners/edges, dents, scuffing, gloss wear
- `6.5-5`: notching, fray, rounding, stains, wrinkles, dents, roughness become obvious
- `4.5-1`: creases, heavy wear, tears, staining, image/text loss, stock damage, glue/tape, structural compromise

### Weighting / Scoring Behavior

- Source docs consistently describe TAG as `compounding`, not averaging
- Lowest significant subgrade heavily influences the final result
- Front defects are described as weighted more than back defects, approximately `1.5x` in the reverse-engineered notes
- Exact proprietary math is not public
- Internal score uses far more granularity than the displayed whole/half grade bands

### DIG / Data Notes

- Every TAG card can include a DIG report with:
  - total score
  - industry grade
  - detailed subgrades
  - DINGS list
  - high-resolution images
  - defect annotations
  - centering metrics
  - population/rank data
- DIG+ / premium tiers in the research docs add more sub-score detail and media

### DINGS

`DINGS` = defects identified of notable grade significance

Common DING buckets in the research docs:

- corner touch / whitening / fraying / softness / missing material
- edge chipping / whitening / fraying / notches
- surface scratch / print line / refractor line / stain / pit / crease / gloss loss
- front or back off-centering

### Practical Data Notes

- The source docs include reverse-engineered point-deduction estimates rather than official published weights
- Example ranges in the research:
  - light corner touch: roughly `-20 to -40`
  - visible corner wear: roughly `-50 to -100`
  - visible edge wear: roughly `-50 to -100`
  - major surface issue: roughly `-150 to -400`
- These are calibration notes, not official TAG disclosures

### Public Rubric Progression

- TAG’s public rubric describes a smooth progression from NHOD-only defects at the top to visible wear, then structural damage, then major tears and image loss at the bottom
- The separate `TAG DIG DATA.md` in this folder provides a full grade-by-grade paraphrase from `1` to `10P`

### Key Notes

- No `9.5`; TAG jumps from `9 Mint` to `10 Gem Mint`
- TCG and sports cards use different back-centering thresholds
- TAG is the most data-rich system in this folder because of the DIG report model

## Cross-Company Quick Comparison

| Company | Top Tier Structure | Subgrades Shown | Scoring Style | Notable Trait |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------|---------------|---------------|
| PSA | Single 10 tier | No | Lowest-factor style | No PSA 9.5 |
