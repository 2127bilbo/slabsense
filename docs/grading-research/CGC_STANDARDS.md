# CGC Card Grading Standards

## Overview
CGC (Certified Guaranty Company) uses a 10-point scale with half-point increments. Unlike BGS, CGC does NOT display subgrades on the label - they assign a single holistic grade.

---

## Grade Scale & Names

| Grade | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 10 | Pristine | Virtually flawless, perfect under 10x magnification. Gold label. |
| 10 | Gem Mint | Nearly perfect, one criterion doesn't meet Pristine standards |
| 9.5 | Mint+ | Premium eye appeal, exceptional centering and surface |
| 9 | Mint | Four sharp corners with minor wear, slight edge flaws, one minor surface defect allowed |
| 8.5 | NM/Mint+ | Relatively smooth edges with minor wear, most original gloss |
| 8 | NM/Mint | Good condition with light play evidence, most original gloss |
| 7.5 | NM- | Near Mint minus |
| 7 | NM | Near Mint |
| 6.5 | EX/MT+ | Excellent/Mint plus |
| 6 | EX/MT | Excellent/Mint |
| 5.5 | EX+ | Excellent plus |
| 5 | EX | Excellent |
| 4.5 | VG/EX+ | Very Good/Excellent plus |
| 4 | VG/EX | Very Good/Excellent |
| 3.5 | VG+ | Very Good plus |
| 3 | VG | Very Good - moderate crease or multiple light creases, rounded corners |
| 2.5 | Good+ | Good plus |
| 2 | Good | Heavy creasing, surface damage, writing allowed |
| 1.5 | Fair | One catastrophic flaw (staple hole, missing surface, severe crease) |
| 1 | Poor | Major surface damage, multiple catastrophic flaws, missing cardstock |

## Centering Requirements

| Grade | Front Centering | Back Centering |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| Pristine 10 | 50/50 (perfect) | 50/50 (perfect) |
| Gem Mint 10 | 55/45 or better | 75/25 or better |
| 9.5 Mint+ | 60/40 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 9 Mint | 60/40 or better | 90/10 or better |
| 8 NM/Mint | 65/35 or better | — |
| 6 Good | 75/25 or better | — |
| 3 VG | 90/10 or better | — |

## Defect Tolerances

### Pristine 10
- Perfect 50/50 centering (front & back)
- Corners: Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ under 10x magnification
- Edges: Perfect condition
- Surface: Free of print spots, perfect gloss, no flaws
- No manufacturing or handling defects
- Flawless color and registration

### Gem Mint 10
- Centering: 55/45 front, 75/25 back
- Corners: Perfect to naked eye, Mint+ under magnification
- Edges: Sharp, minimal wear
- Surface: Free of print spots, perfect gloss
- **Fails ONE Pristine 10 criterion**

### Mint+ (9.5)
- All original gloss
- Four sharp corners
- Slight minor edge flaws may be visible
- One minor spot or surface defect allowed

### Mint (9)
- Four sharp corners with minor wear
- Slight edge flaws
- Original gloss maintained
- One minor surface defect allowed

### NM/Mint (8)
- Relatively smooth edges with minor wear
- Most original border colors and gloss
- Only one minor flaw allowed
- Subtle print spots or focus imperfections acceptable

### Very Good (3)
- One moderate crease OR multiple light creases
- Rounded corners
- Scuffing and scratches visible
- Moderate edge wear
- Loss of original gloss

### Good (2)
- Heavy creasing (edge-to-edge at 2, not edge-to-edge at 2.5)
- Surface damage visible
- Writing on front/back allowed
- Near-miscut or heavy diamond cut allowed

### Poor (1) / Fair (1.5)
- Multiple catastrophic flaws
- Major surface damage
- Missing cardstock portions
- Staple holes, surface-breaking creases

## Special Labels

### Pristine 10 Gold Label
- Unique luxury gold label design
- Reserved exclusively for virtually flawless cards
- Highest designation in CGC system
- Extremely rare

### Gem Mint 10
- Standard 10 grade (not gold label)
- One criterion deviates from Pristine standards

---

## How Final Grade is Calculated

CGC uses a **holistic evaluation** approach (NOT a mathematical formula):

1. Four factors evaluated: Centering, Corners, Edges, Surface
2. Graders consider all factors together
3. **Lowest condition factor typically determines final grade**
4. Single numerical grade assigned (no visible subgrades)
5. For top grades (9.5+), exceptional qualities can elevate grade

**Key difference from BGS**: CGC is holistic; BGS uses mathematical subgrade averaging.
