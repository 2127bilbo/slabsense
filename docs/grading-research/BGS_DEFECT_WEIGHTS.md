# BGS Card Grading - Defect Weights & Scoring Impact

## Overview

BGS (Beckett Grading Services) uses a **weighted algorithm** emphasizing the lowest subgrade. They display **4 subgrades** on every label and are known for being **stricter than PSA**, especially on centering.

---

## The 0.5 Rule (Primary Algorithm)

**Your final BGS grade can only be 0.5 higher than your lowest subgrade**, UNLESS two or more subgrades share the lowest score, in which case the final grade equals the lowest subgrade.

### Examples

| Subgrades (C/Co/E/S) | Final Grade | Explanation |
|----------------------|-------------|-------------|
| 9.5/9.5/9.5/9.0 | **9.5** | Lowest is 9.0, can be 0.5 higher |
| 9.5/9.5/9.0/9.0 | **9.0** | Two subgrades at lowest = final equals lowest |
| 10/10/10/9.5 | **10** | Lowest is 9.5, can be 0.5 higher |
| 10/10/9/10 | **9.5** | Lowest is 9, can be 0.5 higher |
| 10/6/10/10 | **7** | Maximum 2 levels above lowest |

### Exceptions
- This rule works for ~90% of BGS graded cards
- When one subgrade is drastically lower, final score can be higher than 0.5 above lowest
- **Maximum limit:** Final grade rarely exceeds 2 levels above the lowest subgrade

---

## Centering Subgrade Scoring

BGS is **strictest on centering** - requires 50/50 for a perfect 10.

| Subgrade | Front Centering | Back Centering |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **10** | 50/50 (perfect) | 50/50 (perfect) |
| **9.5** | 55/45 or better | 60/40 or better |
| **9** | 60/40 or better | 65/35 or better |
| **8.5** | 62/38 or better | 70/30 or better |
| **8** | 65/35 or better | 75/25 or better |
| **7** | 70/30 or better | 80/20 or better |
| **6** | 75/25 or better | 85/15 or better |

### Example Impact
- Perfect card with 55/45 centering = **Centering subgrade 9.5** → Final grade 10 possible
- Perfect card with 60/40 centering = **Centering subgrade 9** → Final grade 9.5 max

## Corner Subgrade Scoring

| Subgrade | Specifications |
|----------|----------------|
| **10** | All four corners razor sharp, zero imperfections under magnification |
| **9.5** | One corner with slightest imperfection; other three perfect |
| **9** | Minor wear on one or two corners, slight whitening on one acceptable |
| **8.5** | Light wear on multiple corners, slight rounding possible |
| **8** | Clear wear or creases, rounding on one or more corners |
| **7** | Edge/corner whitening visible on close inspection |

### Key Impact
- **Single dinged corner** takes potential 10 down to **8 or lower**
- Any whitening, fraying, or softness automatically drops below 10
- Corner damage is one of the most common reasons cards lose grades

---

## Edge Subgrade Scoring

| Subgrade | Specifications |
|----------|----------------|
| **10** | Flawless, razor-sharp, no chipping/notching/fraying/layering |
| **9.5** | Slight roughness or minor chipping on one edge only |
| **9** | Minor edge wear, slight roughness acceptable |
| **8.5** | Moderate roughness or minor notching acceptable |
| **8** | Noticeable wear, moderate chipping possible |
| **7** | Noticeable roughness, slight notching acceptable |
| **6** | Readily chipped/notched and/or slightly layered |

**Note:** Dark-bordered cards receive stricter scrutiny - whitening shows more obviously.

---

## Surface Subgrade Scoring

The surface is the **most subjective subgrade** and covers scratches, print lines, ink spots, fingerprints, holo scratches, and any surface defects.

| Subgrade | Specifications |
|----------|----------------|
| **10** | Zero print spots, perfect gloss, zero scratches, flawless |
| **9.5** | One minor spot barely noticeable, excellent gloss |
| **9** | A handful of printing specks OR one minor spot, one faint print line allowed |
| **8.5** | Minor print spots, very minor scratches only under close inspection |
| **8** | Few minor print spots, most original gloss, minor scratches under magnification |
| **7** | Little to no scratches visible at 16 inches, most gloss retained |

**Critical:** Surface issues have prevented more 10's than any other issue.

## Black Label vs Gold Label Requirements

### Black Label (BGS 10 Pristine - Quad 10)
- **Requirement:** ALL four subgrades must be perfect 10
- **Centering:** 10 (50/50)
- **Corners:** 10 (razor sharp)
- **Edges:** 10 (flawless)
- **Surface:** 10 (zero defects)
- **Rarity:** Less than 1% of all BGS 10s (~5-6 out of 500-600 submissions)
- **Label:** Black slab with gold lettering
- **Eligibility:** Modern cards only (post-1980)

### Gold Label (BGS 10 Pristine - Standard)
- **Requirement:** Three 10s and one 9.5
- **Any combination:** 10/10/10/9.5 in any category order
- **Rarity:** ~15-20% of BGS 10s
- **Label:** Gold label
- **Eligibility:** All cards (modern and vintage)

## Specific Defect Impacts

### One Tiny Corner Nick
- Drops corner subgrade from 10 to **8 or lower**
- Single dinged corner immediately disqualifies from perfect score

### Light Edge Whitening
- **BGS 9.0:** Light whitening on edges acceptable
- **BGS 8.5:** More noticeable whitening present
- **BGS 8.0:** Clear whitening visible
- Location matters: dark-bordered cards penalized more

### Print Line Visible
- **BGS 10:** Zero print defects allowed
- **BGS 9.5:** Potentially one faint line visible only under magnification
- **BGS 9.0:** One faint, unobtrusive metallic print line allowed
- **BGS 8.5:** Visible print lines but not severe
- Position on card matters (scratch across player's face = severe penalty)

### Factory Print Spot
- Treated as manufacturer defect
- Similar to print lines in impact
- **BGS 9.0:** One faint spot acceptable
- **BGS 8.5:** More visible spots

## BGS 9.5 Subgrade Combinations

For a BGS 9.5 grade, these combinations are possible:

| Subgrades | Scoring |
|-----------|---------|
| 9.5/9.5/9.5/9.0 | 91-92 |
| 10/9.5/9.5/9.0 | 93-94 |
| 10/10/9.5/9.5 | 95-96 |
| All four 9.5s | 96 |
| Three 9.5s + one 10 | Higher |
| Two 9.5s + two 10s | Higher |
| One 9.5 + three 10s | Highest 9.5 |

---

## Combined Defects Impact

### How Defects Compound
- NOT a simple average of four subgrades
- Lowest subgrade heavily weighted
- Multiple low subgrades compound the penalty

### Examples

| Scenario | Subgrades | Final |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| One weak area | 10/10/10/9 | **9.5** |
| Two weak areas | 9.5/9.5/9/9 | **9** |
| One very weak area | 10/10/10/7 | **8.5-9** |
| Mixed strengths | 9.5/9.5/8/9.5 | **8.5** |

## BGS vs Competitors

| Aspect | BGS | PSA | CGC |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|
| Subgrades shown | Yes (4) | No | No |
| Centering for 10 | 50/50 (strictest) | 55/45 | 50/50 (Pristine) |
| Half points | Yes (all) | Yes (no 9.5) | Yes |
| Algorithm | Weighted, 0.5 rule | Lowest factor | Holistic |

---

## Critical Insights

1. **Centering is #1 grade-limiting factor** - More cards lose points to centering than any other attribute
2. **Surface is most subjective** - Evaluated under specific lighting to reveal hidden defects
3. **Corner damage is instantly visible** - Even touching a corner with your finger causes micro-damage
4. **Dark-bordered card edge chipping** is highly penalizing
5. **Weighting system not publicly disclosed** in exact terms
6. **Defect evaluation is qualitative** - Based on visibility and location, not defect counts
