# SGC Card Grading - Defect Weights & Scoring Impact

## Overview

SGC (Sportscard Guaranty Corporation) uses a **"lowest factor"** approach like PSA/BGS, but is known for being **stricter on back centering** than competitors. They have TWO types of 10s: Pristine (Gold Label) and Gem Mint.

---

## Centering Weights & Ratios

**SGC is STRICTER on back centering than competitors** - this is their defining characteristic.

| Grade | Front Centering | Back Centering | Key Difference |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Pristine 10** | 50/50 | 50/50 | Perfect on BOTH sides |
| **Gem Mint 10** | 55/45 | 70/30 | Back is MORE LENIENT |
| **9.5 Mint+** | 55/45 | 55/45 | Equal strictness |
| **9 Mint** | 60/40 | 60/40 | Equal strictness |
| **8.5 NM/MT+** | 65/35 | 65/35 | Equal strictness |
| **8 NM/MT** | 65/35 | 65/35 | Equal strictness |
| **7.5 NM+** | 70/30 | 70/30 | Equal strictness |
| **7 NM** | 70/30 | 70/30 | Equal strictness |

### Why 70/30 Back for Gem Mint?
- Back has simpler artwork/text - minor centering variance less noticeable
- Gem Mint is "standard 10" allowing some tolerance
- Pristine requires perfection on BOTH sides (50/50), making it exceptionally rare
- Reflects practical reality of finding perfectly-centered backs

### Example Impact
A card with perfect front centering (55/45) but bad back centering (75/25) would drop from Pristine to Gem Mint automatically.

## Pristine 10 vs Gem Mint 10

### Pristine 10 (Gold Label)
| Attribute | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Centering** | 50/50 front AND back |
| **Corners** | Four sharp, crisp corners - virtually no visible wear |
| **Edges** | Virtually flawless - very minor fill/fray artifacts ONLY under magnification |
| **Surface** | Flawless - only Non-Human Observable Defects (NHODs) acceptable |
| **Print Quality** | No print or refractor lines visible |
| **Focus** | Crisp focus throughout |
| **Gloss** | Uninterrupted, full original gloss |
| **Stains** | Free of all stains |
| **Visible Defects** | ZERO under normal viewing |

### Gem Mint 10
| Attribute | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Centering** | 55/45 front, 70/30 back |
| **Corners** | Four sharp corners, no visible wear |
| **Edges** | Minor fill/fray acceptable, very minor wear visible only under high-res |
| **Surface** | Slight print spot allowable IF it doesn't detract aesthetically |
| **Print Quality** | "Clean and free from printing defects" |
| **Focus** | Sharp focus |
| **Gloss** | Full original gloss, no breaks |
| **Stains** | Free of stains |

### What Drops Pristine to Gem Mint?
1. Back centering worse than 50/50 (even 51/49 back = Gem Mint)
2. Any visible corner wear (even 1 light touch)
3. Any gloss break (even microscopic)
4. Any visible print line
5. Front centering worse than 50/50

**Most common reason:** Back centering issue (50/50 is extremely difficult)

## Corner/Edge/Surface Scoring

### Corner Wear Progression

| Grade | Corner Condition | Whitening/Microbevel |
|-------|------------------|----------------------|
| **10 Pristine** | Razor sharp, no dings, whitening, fraying | ZERO visible wear |
| **10 Gem Mint** | Four sharp corners, no visible wear | None (must be pristine) |
| **9-9.5** | Four sharp; up to 2 light touches | Minimal/none |
| **8-8.5** | Sharp but touched, some fray | Minor microbevel OK |
| **7-7.5** | Losing sharpness, visible wear | Microbevel present |
| **6-6.5** | Three+ significantly frayed | Fuzzing evident |
| **5-5.5** | All four rounded | Heavy fuzzing |

**Key:** Micro-whitening (tiny white speck from crushing) is the first visible corner wear - appears at 8.5 level or lower.

### Edge Chipping & Wear

| Grade | Edge Condition | Chipping |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| **10 Pristine** | Flawless | ZERO |
| **10 Gem Mint** | Minor fill/fray under hi-res | None |
| **9-9.5** | Minor edge wear | None acceptable |
| **8-8.5** | Minor wear visible | None |
| **7-7.5** | Chipping/fraying visible | Minor |
| **6-6.5** | Minor notches | Slight |
| **5** | Heavy chipping | Present |

**Note:** Layering (card material peeling apart) is a serious defect that drops grade significantly.

### Surface Scratch & Gloss Loss

| Grade | Surface Condition | Scratches | Gloss Loss |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|------------|
| **10 Pristine** | Flawless | ZERO | ZERO breaks |
| **10 Gem Mint** | Extremely attractive | None visible | No breaks |
| **9-9.5** | Few small pits/scratches | Small scratches | Small breaks OK |
| **8-8.5** | Minor scuffing | Light scratches | Breaks acceptable |
| **7-7.5** | Light dents, scuffing | Minor scratching OK | Gloss wear visible |
| **6-6.5** | Light stains/wrinkles | More visible | Noticeable loss |
| **5** | Larger wear areas | Multiple scratches | Gloss lost from surface |

**Key:** At Pristine/Gem Mint, scratches must NOT penetrate the gloss layer.

## Combined Defect Handling

### SGC Uses LOWEST-FACTOR Grading (NOT Averaging)

- **Every deduction is applied**
- **Final grade cannot exceed the lowest significant subgrade**
- **Dramatically lower subgrades heavily weight the result**

### Example Scenarios

| Corners | Edges | Surface | Centering | Result | Why |
|---------|-------|---------|-----------|--------|-----|
| 10 | 10 | 10 | 8.5 | **8.5** | Pulled down by centering |
| 8.5 | 9.5 | 9.5 | 10 | **8.5** | Cannot exceed lowest |
| 9 | 9 | 9 | 10 | **9** | Cannot exceed lowest |
| 9.5 | 9.5 | 9.5 | 9.5 | **Gem Mint 10** | All excellent |

### How Multiple Minor Defects Compound

- **Single minor defect:** -0.5 to -1 grade
- **Two minor defects:** -1 to -1.5 grades (more than sum of parts)
- **Three+ minor defects:** Heavy penalty (can drop 2+ grades)

**Example:** Card with ONE light corner touch = potentially 9.5. Card with light corner touch + minor surface scratch + edge wear = drops to 8.5 (compounding effect).

## Gold Label Criteria

### Gold Label = Pristine 10 Only

Gold label is **EXCLUSIVELY** for Pristine 10 grade. No Gem Mint 10s receive gold labels.

**Requirements:**
- Pristine 10 grade required
- Perfect 50/50 centering FRONT and BACK
- Zero visible defects
- Uninterrupted gloss
- No print or refractor lines
- Only NHODs acceptable

### Rarity Statistics
- **Less than 0.5% of all graded cards** (estimates: 0.2%-0.3%)
- **Approximately 1 in 300 cards** (0.33%)
- Even rarer than in 2017-2023 (standards tightening)

### Market Value
- Gold Label Pristine commands **5-7x premium** over Gem Mint 10
- Example: Gem Mint 10 = $800 → Pristine Gold = $4,000-$5,600+

## Key Differences from Competitors

| Aspect | SGC | PSA | BGS |
|--------|-----|-----|-----|
| Back centering strictness | **Strictest** | Lenient (90/10) | Moderate |
| Two 10 grades | Yes (Pristine/Gem) | No | Yes (Black/Gold) |
| Subgrades shown | No | No | Yes |
| Half points | Yes (1.5-9.5) | Yes (no 9.5) | Yes |
| Gold label | Pristine 10 only | N/A | Multiple 10 types |

---

## Critical Insights

1. **Back centering is SGC's signature strictness** - 70/30 for Gem Mint vs PSA's 90/10
2. **Pristine 10 requires perfection** - less than 0.5% achieve it
3. **Gold label commands huge premium** - 5-7x over Gem Mint
4. **"Lowest factor" approach** - worst area determines grade
5. **Multiple defects compound** - not simple addition
6. **NHODs only for Pristine** - only non-human observable defects allowed
