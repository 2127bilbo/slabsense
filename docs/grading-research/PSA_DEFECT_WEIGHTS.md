# PSA Card Grading - Defect Weights & Scoring Impact

## Overview

PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator) uses a **"lowest score wins"** approach where the single weakest category determines the final grade. They evaluate 4 criteria independently but do NOT publish exact mathematical weights.

---

## The "Lowest Score Wins" Principle

PSA evaluates every card on four criteria: centering, corners, edges, and surface. Each is assessed independently, and the **final grade is essentially the lowest score across those four categories**.

This means:
- A card with near-perfect corners could still be knocked down a grade or two if its edges are significantly chipped or frayed
- The weakest link determines the final grade
- This is NOT an averaging system

---

## Centering Impact

**Updated Standards (Q1 2025):** PSA 10 front centering tightened from 60/40 to 55/45.

| Grade | Front Centering | Back Centering |
|-------|-----------------|----------------|
| **PSA 10** | 55/45 or better | 75/25 or better |
| **PSA 9** | 60/40 or better | 90/10 or better |
| **PSA 8** | 65/35 or better | 90/10 or better |
| **PSA 7** | 70/30 or better | 90/10 or better |
| **PSA 6** | 80/20 or better | 90/10 or better |
| **PSA 5** | 85/15 or better | 90/10 or better |

### Example Impact
- Perfect card with 60/40 centering = **PSA 9 max** (centering caps it)
- Perfect card with 58/42 centering = **PSA 9**
- Perfect card with 70/30 centering = **PSA 7**

## Corner Whitening & Fraying

Corners are one of the most critical factors preventing high grades.

### Grade Impact by Severity

| Defect | Grade Impact |
|--------|--------------|
| **One slight whitening touch** | Drops 10 → **9** minimum |
| **Light fraying on 1-2 corners** | **PSA 9** territory |
| **Multiple corners with fraying** | **PSA 8** range |
| **Moderate fraying on 3+ corners** | **PSA 7-8** range |
| **Accelerated rounding** | **PSA 6** or lower |

### Key Rules
- Any visible whitening prevents PSA 10
- Even tiny whitening hidden by slab usually prevents 10
- Whitening on top/bottom/corners (visible in slab) = **PSA 9** max
- Considerable whitening = **PSA 8 or 7**

### Example
- Perfect card except one corner with slight whitening = **PSA 9** max

---

## Edge Defects

| Defect | Grade Impact |
|--------|--------------|
| **Minor edge wear/chipping** | **PSA 9-8** range |
| **Visible chipping** | **PSA 8** or below |
| **Moderate chipping/fraying** | **PSA 7-6** range |
| **Heavy edge damage** | **PSA 5** or lower |

### Example
- Perfect card except minor edge chipping = **PSA 8-9** range

---

## Surface Defects

Surface issues include scratches, print lines, print spots, stains, and gloss loss.

| Defect | Grade Impact |
|--------|--------------|
| **Single light scratch (magnification only)** | Drops 10 → **PSA 9** |
| **Tiny print spots/specks** | **PSA 9** allows minor specs |
| **Multiple print spots** | **PSA 8-7** |
| **Visible surface scratches** | **PSA 8** and below |
| **Multiple visible scratches** | **PSA 7-6** |
| **Wax staining (slight, back only)** | **PSA 8-9** possible |
| **Visible staining** | **PSA 7-6** |

### Print Lines & Scratches
- Microscopic scratches visible only under magnification = **PSA 9** (not 10)
- Print lines, surface scratches, indentations = common defects preventing 9/10
- These are the most common surface defects that prevent high grades

### Example
- Perfect card except one light scratch = **PSA 9** max

---

## Creases (Automatic Grade Cap)

**Any crease of any size automatically caps at PSA 6 or lower.**

| Crease Type | Grade Cap |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Any crease (no matter size)** | **PSA 6 max** |
| **Light crease** | Typically **PSA 6** |
| **Corner crease** | Often **PSA 5-6** |
| **Heavy crease** | **PSA 4** or lower |

NO creases of any kind are allowed for PSA 10, 9, or 8.

### Example
- Perfect card except light crease = **PSA 6 max**

---

## Combined Defects (How They Compound)

The final grade equals the **weakest of the four categories**.

### Examples

| Subgrades | Final | Why |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| Surface 10, Corners 10, Edges 10, Centering 9 (60/40) | **PSA 9** | Centering caps it |
| Centering 10, Corners 9 (whitening), Edges 10, Surface 10 | **PSA 9** | Corners cap it |
| Centering 10, Corners 10, Edges 8 (chipping), Surface 10 | **PSA 8** | Edges cap it |
| All categories have minor issues | Drops significantly | Compounding effect |

### Two or More Defects Rule
- Multiple defects in SAME category = accelerated drop
- Multiple minor defects across categories = the SINGLE WORST determines grade
- A card with corner + edge + surface issues = grade determined by worst area

## Grade-by-Grade Tolerance Table

| Grade | Centering | Corners | Edges | Surface | Multiple Issues |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **10** | 55/45 | No whitening, sharp | Flawless | Zero scratches, perfect gloss | Zero defects allowed |
| **9** | 60/40 | Slightest touch, minimal fraying | Very clean | One minor flaw under magnification | 1 total defect max |
| **8** | 65/35 | Slight fraying 1-2 corners | Minimal wear | Light scratches, minor print defects | 2-3 minor defects |
| **7** | 70/30 | Visible fraying on some corners | Minor chipping acceptable | Visible wear on close inspection | Multiple minor issues |
| **6** | 80/20 | Significant fraying | Moderate chipping | Obvious wear, visible scratches | Many visible defects |
| **≤5** | 85/15+ | Heavy rounding | Heavy chipping | Extensive damage | Extensive issues |

## PSA 10 vs PSA 9 Comparison

### PSA 10 (Gem Mint)
- Centering: 55/45 or better (front), 75/25 (back)
- Corners: Perfectly sharp, no wear or rounding
- Edges: Crisp and clean, flawless to naked eye
- Surface: Perfect - no scratches, print lines, or gloss loss
- **Zero defects visible**

### PSA 9 (Mint)
- Centering: 60/40 or better
- Corners: Very slight wear, essentially perfect
- Edges: Very minor edge wear if any
- Surface: One minor flaw acceptable (tiny scratch)
- **One minor flaw allowed**

### Key Distinction
PSA 8 allows for **multiple minor flaws**, while PSA 9 should have essentially **just one minor imperfection** visible under close inspection.

## Special Qualifiers

| Qualifier | Meaning | Impact |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **OC** | Off-center (significant centering issues) | Can still get numeric grade |
| **MC** | Miscut (atypical cut) | Reduced value |
| **MK** | Writing, ink marks, pencil marks | Reduced value |
| **ST** | Staining visible | Reduced value |
| **PD** | Print defect (print lines, smudges) | Reduced value |
| **OF** | Out of focus or out of register | Reduced value |

---

## Critical Insights

1. **No published formula exists** - PSA does not publicly disclose exact mathematical weights
2. **Holistic evaluation** - Graders assess all factors together, not as separate point deductions
3. **Centering heavily weighted** - Cards must show 5-10% better centering than minimum requirement
4. **"Weakest link" principle** - Single defect caps overall grade
5. **Whitening is grade-killer** - Even microscopic corner whitening prevents PSA 10
6. **Crease = hard cap** - Creases universally cap at PSA 6 or below
7. **Dark-bordered cards stricter** - Whitening stands out more, penalized harder
